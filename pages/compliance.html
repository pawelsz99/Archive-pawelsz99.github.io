COMPLIANCE TESTING

Compliance testing, also known as compliance testing, is a non-functional testing technique that checks whether a developed system meets the requirements of an organization's standards or not. In simple terms, it is a type of audit that is performed on the system to check whether all specified criteria are met or not. To ensure compliance, each organization sometimes appoints a Board of Regulators and Compliance Experts. This board checks whether development teams meet the standards of the organization or not. Standards typically used in the IT industry are generally defined by large organizations such as IEEE (International Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) or W3C (World Wide Web Consortium).

Objectives

  • Determining that the development and maintenance process is in line with the prescribed methodology.
  • Ensuring that the products of each phase of development comply with the standards, procedures and guidelines.
  • Evaluating the project documentation for completeness and validity

How to check compliance

Compatibility checks are quite simple. A set of standards and procedures is developed and documented for each phase of the development cycle. Compare the results of each stage with the standards and find the gaps. The team can do this through the inspection process, but I would recommend passing this to an independent team. Upon completion of the inspection, the author of each step should receive a list of non-compliant areas that require correction. The inspection process should be rerun after the activity elements have been developed to ensure that the non-conformance items have been approved and closed.
